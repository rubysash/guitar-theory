The Anthem

Pop/Rock Hero

Journey, U2, Beatles

The ultimate "feel-good" loop. High energy, optimistic, and universally loved across generations.

Play Style

Big, open down-strums. Focus on a steady 4/4 driving rhythm.

The Journey

Spanish Descent

Ray Charles, Dire Straits

Dark, mysterious, and driving. This descending minor loop creates immediate narrative tension.

Play Style

Steady thumb-bass on the beat with sharp, percussive finger-snaps.

The Epic

Cinematic Doom

Hans Zimmer, Movie Trailers

Powerful, slow, and massive. Gives the music a "larger than life" sense of scale and importance.

Play Style

Palm-muted power chords. Let the low strings ring out with heavy vibrato.

The Dream

Doo-Wop Love

Earth Angel, Stand By Me

Sentimental, nostalgic, and sweet. The classic sound of the 1950s that everyone knows instantly.

Play Style

Triplet "1-2-3, 1-2-3" strumming. Soft and bouncy acoustic feel.

The Story

Acoustic Hero

Train, Ed Sheeran, Taylor Swift

Warm and narrative. The "Storyteller" loop is the engine of modern hits on the radio.

Play Style

Syncopated "Pop" strumming. Emphasize the backbeat on 2 and 4.

The Garage

Rock & Roll 101

Wild Thing, Louie Louie

Raw, simple, and punchy. The high-voltage foundation of early rock and roll garage bands.

Play Style

Aggressive, fast down-strokes. Keep the muted strings tight and loud.

The Truth

Minor Ballad

Summertime, House of Rising Sun

Deeply emotional, bluesy, and heavy. Used for songs that tell hard-hitting life stories.

Play Style

Slow, rolling arpeggios. Pick individual notes with your fingers or a pick.

The Wedding

Pachelbel Loop

Memories, Canon in D

Stately, repetitive, and majestic. A sequence that builds a sense of tradition and timelessness.

Play Style

Gentle fingerpicking. Focus on smooth transitions between the many chords.

The Riff

Heavy Build

Black Sabbath, White Stripes

Gritty and determined. The sound of a rising storm or a classic blues-rock power riff.

Play Style

Heavy palm-muting on the low strings. Explode on the final chord of the loop.

The Club

ii-V-I Mastery

Autumn Leaves, Blue Bossa

Sophisticated and smooth. The most important resolution in Jazz for creating a professional sound.

Play Style

"Jazz Comping" (Strum on beats 1, 3, or use syncopated 'stabs').

The Delta

12-Bar Shuffle

B.B. King, Chuck Berry

Pure rhythmic fun. The backbone of blues, early rock, and rockabilly. Incredibly versatile.

Play Style

Swing rhythm "DA-da, DA-da." Focus on the 'groove' rather than volume.

The Circle

Circle of 5ths

I Will Survive, Gloria Gaynor

Relentless and dramatic. A loop that never wants to stop, pulling the listener through a journey.

Play Style

Disco-style constant 16th note strumming. Keep the tempo upbeat.

The Ghost

Haunted Ballad

Creep, Wicked Game

Eerie, beautiful, and cinematic. For songs with a lingering sadness and atmosphere.

Play Style

Use lots of reverb. Play slow, sustained chords that fade into each other.

The Tension

Suspense Loop

Phil Collins, The Police

Steady, anxious, and rhythmic. Perfect for creating a sense of anticipation.

Play Style

Muted "Chugging" on a single string between chord changes.

The Streets

Modern Trap

Kendrick Lamar, 21 Savage

Minimalist, dark, and aggressive. Focuses on deep bass and intense rhythmic energy.

Play Style

Short, staccato chords. Focus on the "space" between the notes.

The Amen

Plagal Cadence

Hallelujah, Soul/Gospel

Spiritual, lifting, and warm. The classic sound of resolution, peace, and soulful gospel music.

Play Style

Use an organ-like "swell" (start quiet, then strum harder).

The Shift

Sec. Dominant

Yesterday, Georgia On My Mind

Sophisticated and professional. Adds unexpected harmonic movement that sounds instantly high-end.

Play Style

Delicate arpeggiation. Emphasize the 'unexpected' Major chord in the loop.

The Home

Classic Res

Imagine, Lean On Me

Direct, honest, and nostalgic. The sound of simplicity and melodic truth that everyone can hum along to.

Play Style

Steady 4-beat piano-style strums. Keep it simple and heartfelt.

The Rise

Uplifting Minor

Sweet Dreams, Zombie

Dark energy with constant forward motion. Powerful and driving without losing its minor-key edge.

Play Style

Aggressive eighth-note chugging. Great for distortion.

The Lounge

Jazz Subdominant

Girl From Ipanema, Jazz Pop

Light, breezy, and summer-ready. A perfect "walk through the park" sound with a touch of class.

Play Style

Bossa Nova rhythm (1 - and-2 - and - 4). Use light, clean tones.
